CREATORS OF ROAD RUNNER.

Chuck Jones.

“ANIMATION ISN’T THE ILLUSION OF LIFE; IT IS LIFE.”

In a career spanning over 60 years, Jones made more than 300 animated films, winning three Oscars as director and in 1996 an honorary Oscar for Lifetime Achievement.

Career

  • DIRECTOR, WARNER BROS. ANIMATION (1938-1962)
  • HEAD OF MGM ANIMATION DEPARTMENT (1963-1971)
  • OF CHILDREN’S PROGRAMMING ABC-TV (1972-1973)
  • CEO AND PRESIDENT OF CHUCK JONES ENTERPRISES (1962-2002)
  • CHUCK JONES FILM PRODUCTIONS (1993-1997)

Micheal Maltese

Michael Maltese (February 6, 1908 – February 22, 1981) was an American storyman for classic animated cartoon shorts. He is best known for working in the 1950s on a series of Merrie Melodies cartoons with director Chuck Jones.

Career

  • In 1941, Maltese was hired by Leon Schlesinger Productions, which three years later became Warner Bros. Cartoons, Inc.
  • Maltese was also the voice of the Lou Costello-esque character in Wackiki Wabbit (1943).
